Introduction to Forex: Understanding Currency Pairs

Welcome to the first lesson of the Forex Fundamentals course. In this lesson, we'll explore the foundational concept of currency pairs — the building blocks of every Forex trade.

What is a Currency Pair?

A currency pair is the quotation of two different currencies, where one is quoted against the other. For example, EUR/USD represents the Euro priced against the US Dollar. Every Forex trade involves simultaneously buying one currency and selling another.

💡 Key Concept: The first currency in a pair is the "base currency" and the second is the "quote currency." When EUR/USD = 1.0847, it means 1 Euro buys 1.0847 US Dollars.

Major Currency Pairs

The most traded pairs involve the US Dollar (USD) and are called "major pairs." These include EUR/USD, GBP/USD, USD/JPY, and USD/CHF. They offer the tightest spreads and highest liquidity — ideal for beginners to start with.

Minor pairs (or "crosses") don't include USD but involve major currencies like EUR/GBP or GBP/JPY. Exotic pairs combine a major currency with one from an emerging market, such as USD/TRY.

Reading a Forex Quote

Every currency pair has a bid price (what buyers pay) and an ask price (what sellers receive). The difference is the "spread" — this is how most Forex brokers make their profit. Tighter spreads mean lower trading costs for you.
